Scientists assume that it began with the formation of two large stars (super giants) that burn quickly after collapsing to form a blue multi-star star that breaks up to form many stars.

Space phenomena

METEORITE

Tuesday  , October 14 1969

Meteorites are space objects made of minerals and rocks that come from outer space and enter the Earth's atmosphere.They provide valuable information about the history of the formation of the solar system, which led scientists to discoverhow the planets formed. Meteorites range in size from small grains to giant rocks. One of the most famous meteorites is the Chelyabinsk meteor, which entered the Earth's atmosphere on February 15, 2013, and fell over Russia, causing severe damage.

SINNER

Wednesday  , October 25 1967

They are icy and rocky bodies that orbit the sun in long, elliptical orbits. The ice evaporates when the comet approaches the sun. There are 3 types of comets: Short-period comets: orbit in short orbits of 200 years, such as Halley's Comet. Long-period comets: require thousands of years to complete an orbit around the sun. Comets affect the planets they approach, changing the composition of the atmosphere or even introducing new water and salts. It is believed that they may have brought water and organic compounds to Earth.

METEOR

Saturday  , November 12 1833

They are small particles of rocks or minerals that move in space and sometimes enter the Earth's atmosphere. They come from the remains of asteroids and comets and their sizes range from a small grain of sand to a small piece. When a meteor enters the atmosphere at high speeds, friction occurs between them and air molecules, which leads to their ignition.A meteor shower occurs when the Earth passes through the path of the comet's remains. Meteors can be a source of organic compounds and water that helped form life.

Aurora Borealis

Tuesday  , May 20 1963

Known as the Northern and Southern Lights, it is one of the most amazing natural phenomena. It occurs as a result of the interaction of charged particles coming from the sun with the atmosphere. It occurs at the poles and places close to them during the winter months during high solar activity. It is not a continuous phenomenon, but rather occurs in varying cycles according to solar activity, accompanied by faint sounds that are rarely heard. The colors we see depend on the type of interacting molecules.
